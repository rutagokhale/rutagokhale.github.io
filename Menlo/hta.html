HTA at Menlo

Menlo Innovations is a custom software design and development company. My work as a High-Tech Anthropologist® (HTA)involves being a user researcher, UX designer, business analyst and a consultant! I work in cross-functional teams on a day-to-day basis in a Lean and Agile model to produce client deliverables. I collaborate with software developers, project managers and QAs to design and implement software features.

As an HTA at Menlo, my two key takeaways have been:

  • Pain can be a symptom of a problem. Find the root cause of pain to discover the actual problem that needs a solution.
  • Changing or building new software won’t change user behaviors. The solution to a problem may not always be a digital tool.

Skills and methods that I practice as an HTA:

  • Interviews and Observations
    I gather information about existing or potential users of an application by doing unstructured interviews and observing behaviors in their native environments.

  • Data synthesis and project scoping
    I synthesize all the collected information and data using a mind map. This synthesized information is also useful to outline the scope of the problem that would be addressed.

  • User Personas and Persona Map
    I create personas to determine target users, and also highlight the secondary and tertiary users of the software application. This tool is useful to identify the primary market that the application would be serving.

  • UX design
    I design interactions and wireframes to build features in an application, so that the primary persona can achieve the intended tasks.

  • Usability Testing
    I test my designs with real users so as to get feedback and iterate on my designs to make them more usable and also serve the business goals of the application.

  • Visual Design
    I design graphics and visuals for wireframes so that the application can better appeal to the users. I also create promotional and marketing materials for Menlo’s workshops and other events.
